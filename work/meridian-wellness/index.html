Case Study

Meridian Wellness

A wellness practice rebuilt so the brand finally matched the quality of the work behind it.

Industry Wellness
Services Brand Identity
Web Design
Year 2024

The Client

A practice that had outgrown its brand.

Meridian Wellness is a wellness practice built on the belief that good health is earned through intention, not accident. The team had spent years developing a reputation for careful, considered work with their clients. They were known by the people who had experienced what they did. The problem was what happened before that point.

When a potential client encountered Meridian for the first time, online or otherwise, they were not meeting the practice at its best. They were meeting a brand that had not kept pace with the work. And a brand that does not reflect the quality of what it represents costs the business in every moment that matters.

The Problem

A brand assembled in pieces.

When a potential client encountered Meridian online, they were met with a visual identity that had been patched together over time. The logo from one era, the website from another, the print materials from a third. No single element was broken. But nothing worked together either.

The brand communicated effort without communicating intention. For a practice built on trust, expertise, and a particular kind of attention, that incoherence was costing them in the moments that mattered most. The first impression should have done the qualifying. Instead it was raising questions the practice then had to answer in person.

Direction

Calm, considered, and built to hold together.

The brief was to build a brand that communicates confidence without coldness. Meridian's work is human and precise, and the visual system needed to carry both of those qualities at once. Not clinical. Not soft. Something that felt like a practice that takes what it does seriously without making that feel inaccessible.

We started from the mark and worked outward, making sure every decision in color, type, structure, and space was something the whole system could sustain. The goal was not to make Meridian look different. It was to make it finally look like itself.

The Insight

The work had already earned the trust. The brand just needed to show it.

What already existed at Meridian was excellent. The practice, the people, and the quality of the work. What it needed was a visual system that communicated that before anyone walked through the door.

Brand Identity

A system built to scale.

The identity work covered the full range. A refined logo mark grounded in restraint and clarity. A color palette that communicates calm authority without reading as sterile. A typographic system with enough hierarchy to carry the full range of the practice's communications. Every element was tested against the contexts that mattered most to Meridian: digital, print, and the clinical environments where the brand had to work without drawing attention to itself.

The work was delivered with a brand guidelines document that gave the team everything they needed to use the system consistently without external support. Not a file dump. A working reference for a brand they could own and extend.

Brand Identity

Web Design

A site that leads with the practice.

The site was designed around a single question: what does a prospective client need to understand before they decide to reach out? The structure was built from that answer inward. Clear hierarchy, deliberate pacing, and a visual language that carried the new brand without competing with the content.

Someone who was not the right fit should know that quickly. Someone who was the right fit should feel it. The site now functions as a first impression and a qualification tool at once, working for Meridian before anyone on the team has had a chance to speak.

The Outcome

One brand. One business.

Meridian launched the new brand and website together. The practice now presents consistently across every surface it touches. The identity, site, printed materials, and digital communications all work from the same visual language. The team can extend and manage the system without it drifting from what was built.

What existed before as a collection of assembled pieces exists now as one coherent thing. The brand reflects the practice. The first impression reflects the quality of the work. And the conversations that matter most start from a stronger position.

A cohesive visual identity

Every element from the logo to the brand guidelines was built to work together and hold up over time, across every surface the practice uses.

A site that works as a first impression

The website now reflects the quality of the practice and qualifies the right visitors before anyone on the team has to get involved.

A system the team can manage

Delivered with documentation and training so the practice can maintain and extend the brand independently without it drifting.
